Pro tip

Train/test ratio will depend on the size of your data. If you’re dealing with big data, you can afford a larger percent for training. For small data, it’s common to have a 70/30 split, but if you have 10 million training examples, it’s not uncommon to have a ratio on the order of 95/5. Ultimately, you need enough data in your test set to be confident in your evaluation, but you want to reserve the rest of the data for training the model.

Student correctly identifies whether the model at a max depth of 1 and a max depth of 10 suffer from either high bias or high variance, with justification using the complexity curves graph.

Correct, low training and validation curves signal that our model is failing to learn from the data due to high bias. A large gap between the training curve and validation curve signal that our model is fitting well to the training data but failing to generalize to unseen data.

When diagnosing bias, we compare the training score to some benchmark value to ask the question "how well is our model learning?" When diagnosing variance, we compare our training score to the validation score to ask the question "how well does our model generalize what it learns?"

Feel free to check out this superb blog post for more on bias and variance.

Evaluating Model Performance

Student correctly describes the grid search technique and how it can be applied to a learning algorithm.

Student correctly describes the k-fold cross-validation technique and discusses the benefits of its application when used with grid search when optimizing a model.

Nice explanation here.

Using K-folds cross validation, we partition the training dataset into K bins. We then run K different learning experiments, each time choosing one bin as the validation subset while using the rest to train our algorithm. We can then average the results from all of the experiments to get an accurate picture of its performance using the entire training dataset to both train and validate the performance of our algorithm.

By using k-fold cross validation we can also ensure that we haven’t overfit our hyperparameters to a specific validation subset.
